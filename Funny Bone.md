Legends say the funny bone was carved from the remains of fearsome Grebletorious, king of dragons, eater of the seven plane's cousin, Doofly the Plunderful. Doofly was given away by Grebletorious as a peace offering to his enemies, only to tell a joke so terrible it brought the kingdom to ruins.

The hefty club, this simple, two-handed weapon deals 1d8 bludgeoning damage, but on a successful hit does something quite ... humorous.  The target must succeed on a wisdom saving throw of 15 or fall onto the ground in a fit of laughter, but only if the wielder of the funny bone tells a joke. If the joke is absolutely dreadful (enough to make the entire table groan.) The target also takes 1d4 psychic damage.

After a failed save, the target is knocked prone and will spend their next turn laughing and rolling around on the ground. They make a saving throw at the beginning of each turn. Yadayada.
